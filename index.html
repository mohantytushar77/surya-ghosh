Getting startedHow much emergency fund do I actually need?▾
Surya's answer· Answered Sunday

Six months of your essential expenses — rent, food, EMIs, school fees — kept in a savings account or liquid fund. Not investments, not gold: money you can reach in a day. If your job is unstable or you're the only earner, stretch it to nine months. Build this before any investing; it's what lets you invest without fear later.

SIP & investingSIP vs FD — what's actually better?▾
Surya's answer· Answered Sunday

Wrong question — they do different jobs. An FD protects money you'll need in the next 1–3 years. A SIP into an index fund builds money you won't touch for 7+ years, riding through market ups and downs. FDs feel safe but often lose to inflation after tax; equity feels risky but historically wins over long periods. Use both, for different goals. Anyone who says one is always better is selling something.

TaxOld tax regime or new — how do I choose?▾
Surya's answer· Answered Sunday

One honest rule: calculate, don't assume. If you genuinely use deductions — home loan interest, 80C investments, health insurance, HRA — the old regime can still win. If you don't, the new regime's lower rates usually win and your life gets simpler. Run both numbers on the official income-tax calculator each year; it takes ten minutes and the answer can flip as your salary changes.

FIREIs early retirement realistic on an Indian salary?▾
Surya's answer· Answered Sunday

Yes — but honesty first: it depends far more on your savings rate than your salary. Someone earning ₹60,000 and investing ₹20,000 will reach freedom before someone earning ₹2 lakh and investing ₹15,000. The path is unglamorous: raise your savings rate every increment, automate SIPs, avoid lifestyle debt, and give it 15–20 patient years. Use the calculator above with your real numbers — that's your honest starting point.

Getting startedI have EMIs. Should I invest or clear debt first?▾
Surya's answer· Answered Sunday

Look at the interest rate. Credit cards (30–40%) and personal loans (12–18%) — attack these first; no honest investment reliably beats those rates. A home loan at 8–9% is different: keep it, take the tax benefit, and invest alongside it. And whatever your debt, build one month of emergency money first, so a surprise doesn't push you into new debt.
